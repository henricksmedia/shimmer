Quick start

                

                    What is shimmer? It is the thin, fizzy, metallic noise
                    that AI music tools leave in the high end of your track. You
                    hear it as sizzle on cymbals, a glassy sheen on vocals, or a
                    busy hiss up top. It is not part of your music — the
                    model added it. Shimmer finds that noise, turns it down, and
                    leaves your song alone.
                


                
Your first master, step by step

                
    
                    
  1. 1
                        Drop your track in. WAV, MP3, FLAC, OGG, or M4A all work.
    2. 
                    
  2. 2
                        Click Analyze. Shimmer listens to your song, checks it
                        against all 19 presets, and picks the best match. It tells you
                        why, and jumps the preview loop to the spot where the noise is
                        worst.
    3. 
                    
  3. 3
                        Listen. A short section loops on its own. Press
                        2 to hear the cleaned version, 1 to hear your
                        original. Switch back and forth — that is the only way
                        to really judge it.
    4. 
                    
  4. 4
                        Adjust if you need to. Pick a different preset and you
                        hear the change in about a second. Use Preset strength
                        to dial it up or down.
    5. 
                    
  5. 5
                        Click Clean & Master, then download from the green
                        banner. That is your finished, release-ready file.
    6. 
                


                

                    The one habit worth building: press 3 and listen to
                    the Removed track. That is only the noise Shimmer is taking
                    out, turned up so you can hear it. It should sound like hiss, fizz,
                    and sizzle — nothing else. If you hear vocals, snare hits, or
                    melody in there, you are cutting into your music. Lower the
                    Preset strength until the music disappears from it.
                


                
Good to know

                
    
                    
  • Volume fools your ears. Louder almost always sounds
                    better, even when it is not. Loudness-matched A/B is on by
                    default so you compare the sound, not the level.
    • 
                    
  • Your low end is never touched. Kick, bass, and the body
                    of your vocals bypass the cleaning completely. Shimmer only works
                    above about 4.5 kHz.
    • 
                    
  • Nothing is permanent until you export. Presets, sliders,
                    and EQ only change what you hear in the preview. Your original file
                    is never modified.
    • 
                


                

                    Tip: click the ? next to
                    any control for help on that one knob.

Troubleshooting

                

                    Find the problem you can actually hear, then follow the fix.
                    Always compare against your Original (key 1) and
                    listen to the Removed track (key 3). If you hear
                    real music in Removed, you are cutting too hard — that is
                    the single most useful check in the whole app.
                


                
I still hear the noise after cleaning


                

                    
A "shhhhh" that rides on both the vocals and the cymbals

                    
What is happening: you have two problems at once. There
                    is a glassy sheen baked into the vocal (2–8 kHz), and
                    separately there is fizz up in the top end (5–12 kHz). A
                    preset built for one will only clean half of it. This is the most
                    common complaint about Suno tracks.

                    
Fix: use Vocal Glaze + Top End. If some sizzle
                    survives, raise Preset strength to 150–200%. Still
                    there? Open Advanced artifact controls and push
                    De-harsh up past 85%, then pull Air cut down to
                    about −3 dB.

                


                

                    
The vocal itself sounds glassy or plastic

                    
What is happening: the shimmer is not sitting around the
                    voice — it is the voice. The model rendered the
                    vocal's overtones too bright, so ordinary noise removal cannot tell
                    the two apart.

                    
Fix: use Vocal Glaze. It reads the clean, low part
                    of the voice (300–1500 Hz) to work out what should stay, then
                    tames everything above it. If the top end is also fizzy, use
                    Vocal Glaze + Top End instead.

                


                

                    
The noise follows the music and disappears in the gaps

                    
What is happening: the shimmer swells with every note and
                    dies the moment the music stops. It is not constant, so noise
                    removal never gets a quiet moment to learn from. It is not
                    rhythmic either, so flicker tools miss it.

                    
Fix: use Echo Sheen. It is the only preset that
                    reaches into the space right after each note to catch that
                    lingering tail.

                


                

                    
A smooth, airy wash in the upper mids

                    
What is happening: a haze sitting in the 3–8 kHz
                    presence range — the band where vocals and guitars cut
                    through a mix. It appears with the music and vanishes in silence.

                    
Fix: use Presence Haze. It tracks the noise floor
                    quickly enough to catch a wash that only exists while the music is
                    playing.

                


                

                    
A washy, metallic ring behind everything

                    
What is happening: a cymbal-like "shhhh" between 4 and
                    10 kHz that sounds like a layer nobody played. It hides in the busy
                    parts of your track, which is exactly where most tools stop
                    working.

                    
Fix: use Phantom Cymbal. It keeps working during
                    dense, loud sections instead of backing off.

                


                

                    
The whole track sounds harsh and tiring

                    
What is happening: a gritty, distortion-like texture
                    across 4–12 kHz. It sounds painful rather than washy, and it
                    wears your ears out after a minute.

                    
Fix: use Harsh Veil. This is the aggressive
                    option — heavy harshness control plus fast noise removal.

                


                

                    
I tried a preset at 200% and it still bothers me

                    
What is happening: either it is the wrong preset for what
                    you actually have, or your track has three or more problems
                    stacked on top of each other.

                    
Fix, in this order:

                    
    
                        
  1. Take the Pick a preset quiz again with fresh ears.
                        What you hear now may point somewhere different.
    2. 
                        
  2. Click Analyze on the Master screen. If it suggests
                        something else, try that.
    3. 
                        
  3. Last resort: Deep Scrub. Every tool at full
                        strength, run twice. Expect to lose some top-end air —
                        it is a trade, and you should A/B carefully before you commit.
    4. 
                    

                


                
I cut too much — now something sounds wrong


                

                    
The result sounds dull or lifeless

                    
What is happening: you are cutting too much out of the
                    top end, or the cleaning is reaching higher than it needs to.

                    
Fix: lower Preset strength first — that
                    solves it most of the time. If you are in Advanced artifact
                    controls: raise Threshold, lower Slope, drop
                    Mix to 70–80%, or pull End Hz down a couple
                    thousand.

                


                

                    
Vocals sound lispy, or the "s" sounds went missing

                    
What is happening: the harshness control is eating your
                    consonants along with the noise. The "s", "t", and "ch" sounds are
                    short bursts of high frequency — they look a lot like
                    artifacts to a detector.

                    
Fix: turn De-harsh down. If you need it high for
                    the artifact, try Sibilance Rattle instead, which is built
                    to target the rattle without flattening the consonant.

                


                

                    
The bass or kick feels thin

                    
What is happening: this should be rare — the low
                    end bypasses cleaning entirely. If it is happening, Start Hz
                    has been pulled too far down into the mids, or Denoise and
                    De-resonator are turned up high enough to bleed downward.

                    
Fix: raise Start Hz — shimmer never lives
                    in the bass — then turn Denoise and
                    De-resonator back down.

                


                

                    
Cymbals lost their sparkle

                    
What is happening: the cleaning is reaching up into the
                    air band where your cymbals live, or Air cut is pulling the
                    top down too far.

                    
Fix: lower End Hz a little, and move Air cut
                    back toward 0 dB. Dropping Preset strength to 70–85%
                    also works well here.

                


                

                    
A warble, pumping, or "underwater" sound appeared

                    
What is happening: the noise removal is working too hard
                    or reacting too fast, so it is adding its own artifact on top of
                    the one you were trying to remove.

                    
Fix: turn Denoise down. This is the one control
                    where less is almost always better — try halving it and
                    listen again.

                


                

                    
The Removed track has real music in it

                    
What is happening: the detector is too sensitive and is
                    treating parts of your song as noise. This is the most important
                    problem in this list, because you will not always hear the damage
                    on the Processed track — but it is there.

                    
Fix: lower Preset strength until Removed sounds
                    like hiss and fizz only. In Advanced, raise Threshold or
                    lower Slope.

                


                
Common questions


                

                    
I want more cleanup than 200% strength

                    
Strength stops at 200% on purpose — past that, damage to
                    your music is close to guaranteed. If you truly need more:

                    
    
                        
  1. Open Advanced artifact controls and raise the
                        individual tools that match what you hear (for example
                        De-harsh to 95%).
    2. 
                        
  2. Try Deep Scrub, which already runs everything at
                        maximum, twice.
    3. 
                        
  3. Export the cleaned file, then run it through Shimmer a
                        second time. Two gentle passes usually sound better than one
                        brutal one.
    4. 
                    

                


                

                    
Which preset should I start with?

                    
Two ways to get there:

                    
    
                        
  1. Click Analyze on the Master screen. Shimmer scores
                        all 19 presets against your track and picks the best fit.
    2. 
                        
  2. Use the Pick a preset tab and answer a couple of
                        questions about what you hear.
    3. 
                    

                    
Both are just starting points. Trust your ears over either one.

                


                

                    
My track has two problems at once

                    
That is normal. Use a preset built for it:

                    
    
                        
  • Vocal Glaze + Top End — glassy vocals plus
                        top-end sizzle. The most common pairing by far.
    • 
                        
  • Deep Scrub — for three or more problems
                        stacked together.
    • 
                    

                


                

                    
My export is quieter than other songs

                    
That is correct behaviour, not a bug. Streaming
                    (−14 LUFS) is the level Spotify and Apple Music
                    normalize to. If you master louder, they simply turn you back
                    down — and you gave up dynamic range for nothing.

                    
Use Loud (−11) only when you need punch for a
                    playlist or a club system, and CD / Club (−9) only if
                    you know exactly why you need it.

                

            


            

                
Setup

                

                    Shimmer works out of the box for most files. Two features need
                    a one-time extra install.
                


                
MP3 and M4A files

                

                    WAV, FLAC, and OGG work right away. MP3, M4A, and AAC need a
                    free helper called ffmpeg — it is the tool that
                    reads and writes compressed audio.
                


                

                    
Windows

                    
Open a terminal and run:

                    
winget install ffmpeg

                    
Then restart Shimmer.

                


                

                    
macOS and Linux

                    
macOS: brew install ffmpeg

                    Linux: apt install ffmpeg

                    
Or download from
                        ffmpeg.org
                        and add it to your PATH.
                    

                


                
Stem separation (the Remix tab)


                

                    
First run downloads the separation engine

                    
Splitting a song into vocals, drums, bass, and other needs a
                    large one-time download (several GB). Shimmer installs it in its
                    own separate folder so it never interferes with the rest of the
                    app.

                    
If you have an NVIDIA graphics card, separation uses it
                    automatically and runs much faster. Without one it still works,
                    just slower. Results are saved per track, so you only ever wait
                    once for a given song.

                

            


            

                
About Shimmer


                

                    Shimmer by The Treq — a free, offline tool that
                    removes AI generation artifacts and masters your track for
                    release. Built by an AI music creator who got tired of hearing
                    that fizz on his own tracks.
                


                

                    
Your music stays on your computer

                    
Everything runs on your own machine. Your tracks are never
                    uploaded, never sent to a server, and never used to train
                    anything. No account, no subscription, and no internet needed
                    once it is installed.

                


                

                    
No AI in the cleanup

                    
It sounds backwards for a tool aimed at AI music, but the
                    cleaning engine uses classic signal processing — the same
                    kind of math behind an EQ or a compressor. That means the same
                    settings always give you the same result. Nothing is guessed,
                    and nothing changes between runs.
