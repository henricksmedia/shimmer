Quick start

                

                    What is shimmer? A thin, fizzy, metallic noise that AI
                    music tools add to the high end. Shimmer finds that noise and
                    turns it down, leaving your music alone.
                


                
How to use it

                
    
                    
  1. 1 Drop or pick an audio file.
    2. 
                    
  2. 2 Click Analyze (preset + loudness).
    3. 
                    
  3. 3 Click Clean & Master.
    4. 
                    
  4. 4 Compare with keys 1/2/3 (use loudness-matched A/B).
    5. 
                    
  5. 5 Hit Download in the green banner.
    6. 
                


                

                    Play the Removed track. It should sound like fizz or
                    hiss. If you hear music in it, you cut too hard — raise
                    Threshold or lower Slope.

Which preset should I use?

                

                    Answer a couple of questions and we'll pick one for you.
                    Or click Auto Detect on the main panel to let
                    Shimmer listen to your file and decide.
                

                

            


            

                
What each control does

                

                    The preset already sets these for you. Tweak only if
                    something sounds off. Each card tells you when to turn
                    a knob up or down.
                

                

            


            

                
Troubleshooting

                

                    Each card is a symptom you can hear. It tells you the
                    root cause, which preset fits best, and which sliders
                    to nudge if the preset alone doesn't get you all the
                    way there. Always A/B against the Original and
                    listen to the Removed track — if Removed
                    has actual music in it, you're cutting too hard.
                


                
I still hear shimmer / hiss after running a preset


                

                    
"shhhhh" sound that rides with vocals AND cymbals (most common Suno complaint)

                    
Root cause: two artifacts at once —
                    shimmer baked into vocal harmonics (2–8 kHz)
                    PLUS the AM-modulated diffusion residual in the
                    brilliance band (5–12 kHz). Single-target
                    presets clean half and miss the other half.

                    
Try: Vocal Glaze + Top End. If
                    still present, raise Preset Strength toward
                    150–200%, then push the De-harsh slider
                    higher (0.85+). If brilliance still sizzles, also
                    nudge Air cut to about −3 dB.

                


                

                    
Shimmer sits ON the vocal itself, not after it

                    
Root cause: the AI rendered the vocal
                    overtones (formants F2–F5, 2–8 kHz) too
                    bright. The shimmer IS the vocal harmonics, so
                    broadband denoise and peak-based shimmer
                    suppression cannot separate it.

                    
Try: Vocal Glaze (or Vocal
                    Glaze + Top End if there's also brilliance
                    sizzle). It uses DeHarsh anchored to the clean
                    vocal fundamental (300–1500 Hz) as a vocal-
                    aware shimmer compressor — the only preset
                    that does this.

                


                

                    
Shimmer follows the music and vanishes in silence

                    
Root cause: signal-correlated shimmer
                    that rises and falls with the content envelope. Not
                    constant (denoise misses it because there are no
                    "quiet" frames where it's audible), not periodic
                    (flicker tools miss it).

                    
Try: Echo Sheen. It's the only
                    preset that engages the downward expander to push
                    down 3–10 kHz energy whenever the music drops
                    below threshold — catching the shimmer tail
                    that lingers at the edges of musical events.

                


                

                    
Smooth airy wash in the upper-mids

                    
Root cause: a noise-like haze in the
                    3–8 kHz presence band that appears with the
                    music and vanishes in silence. Default denoise
                    misses it because its noise-floor estimator
                    averages over quiet frames where the wash isn't
                    present.

                    
Try: Presence Haze. It uses a
                    short minimum-statistics window so the floor
                    estimate tracks the content-gated wash.

                


                

                    
Washy, metallic, ringy "shhhhh"

                    
Root cause: resonant-but-broadband
                    artifacts in 4–10 kHz that have a faint
                    cymbal-like quality. DeResonator alone misses it
                    because its per-frame density gate fails on dense
                    brilliance frames.

                    
Try: Phantom Cymbal —
                    DeResonator and DeHarsh with lowered thresholds
                    and a high density floor so they fire on dense
                    frames.

                


                

                    
Harsh, gritty texture across the upper-mids

                    
Root cause: distortion-like grit
                    spanning 4–12 kHz, often from
                    over-aggressive AI upsampling. Sounds painful
                    rather than washy.

                    
Try: Harsh Veil. Aggressive
                    DeHarsh with a very low threshold plus
                    responsive denoise.

                


                

                    
Tried a preset, raised strength to 200%, still bothered

                    
Root cause: either the wrong preset for
                    the artifact shape, OR a multi-artifact track that
                    needs a kitchen-sink approach.

                    
Try (in order):

                    
    
                        
  1. Re-take the Pick a preset quiz with
                        fresh ears — the symptom may match a
                        different preset.
    2. 
                        
  2. Run Auto Detect; if it suggests
                        something different, try that.
    3. 
                        
  3. Last resort: Deep Scrub. It runs
                        every stage at maximum across 3–18 kHz
                        with 2 iterations. Expect some loss of top-end
                        air as a tradeoff.
    4. 
                    

                


                
I cut too much / something sounds wrong


                

                    
The result sounds dull or lifeless

                    
Root cause: over-cutting in the brilliance
                    band, or the End Hz extends too far up.

                    
Fix: raise Threshold, lower
                    Slope, drop Mix to 70–80%, OR
                    drop End Hz a couple thousand Hz.

                


                

                    
Vocals sound lispy or consonants weakened

                    
Root cause: De-harsh is too aggressive
                    and is eating the consonant transients ("s", "t",
                    "ch") along with the artifact.

                    
Fix: lower De-harsh, raise
                    dh_thr_db (in overrides) by 1–2 dB,
                    or shorten dh_release_ms.

                


                

                    
Bass or kick feels thin

                    
Root cause: Start Hz is reaching
                    too far down into the mids, or
                    Denoise/De-resonator are too high
                    and bleeding into the low band.

                    
Fix: raise Start Hz (shimmer
                    never lives in the bass), then turn down
                    Denoise and De-resonator.

                


                

                    
Cymbals or top-end air dulled

                    
Root cause: End Hz is reaching
                    too high, or Air cut is too aggressive.

                    
Fix: lower End Hz a little, and
                    move Air cut back toward 0 dB.

                


                

                    
Weird warble, pumping, or "underwater" sound

                    
Root cause: denoise too aggressive or
                    too fast — it's adding its own artifacts.

                    
Fix: lower Denoise, or in
                    overrides lengthen dn_minwin_ms and
                    dn_release_ms for smoother tracking.

                


                

                    
The Removed track has actual music in it

                    
Root cause: detection threshold is too
                    loose and the tool is grabbing real signal as if
                    it were artifact.

                    
Fix: raise Threshold or lower
                    Slope until the Removed track sounds like
                    just hiss / fizz / shimmer with no melodic
                    content audible.

                


                
Workflow questions


                

                    
I want more cleanup than 200% Preset Strength

                    
Preset Strength caps at 200% to keep things
                    safe. To go further, switch to per-slider
                    overrides:

                    
    
                        
  1. Push the primary tools higher (e.g.
                        De-harsh to 0.95, Denoise to
                        0.7).
    2. 
                        
  2. Add a second iteration via
                        iterations in overrides.
    3. 
                        
  3. Stack a second preset by exporting the
                        cleaned file and processing it again.
    4. 
                    

                


                

                    
How do I know which preset to start with?

                    
Two paths:

                    
    
                        
  1. Click Auto Detect on the main
                        panel — Shimmer scores all 16 presets
                        against your file and picks the highest.
    2. 
                        
  2. Use the Pick a preset tab to walk
                        through a short decision tree based on what
                        you actually hear.
    3. 
                    

                


                

                    
Two artifacts at once (e.g. vocal glaze + top sizzle)

                    
Use the combo presets:

                    
    
                        
  • Vocal Glaze + Top End —
                        vocal-anchored shimmer + Suno hash in the
                        brilliance band (most common combo).
    • 
                        
  • Deep Scrub — everything-on
                        for 3+ overlapping artifacts.
    • 
                    

                

            


            

                
Setup for MP3 and M4A files

                

                    WAV, FLAC, and OGG files work out of the box. MP3 and
                    M4A need a free helper called ffmpeg.
                


                

                    
Install ffmpeg on Windows

                    
Open a terminal and run:

                    
winget install ffmpeg

                    
Then restart Shimmer.

                


                

                    
Other systems

                    

                        Download from
                        ffmpeg.org
                        and add it to your PATH.
